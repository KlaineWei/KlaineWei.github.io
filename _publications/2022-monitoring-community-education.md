## Abstract

This work presents a data-driven model for monitoring and evaluating the development of community education, using Jiangsu Province as a case study. We design a multi-dimensional indicator system that captures resource allocation, participation, and outcomes, and propose a decision-making pipeline that turns the collected indicators into actionable insights for regional planning. The model is applied to real survey and administrative data from Jiangsu, and the results illustrate how data-driven monitoring can surface structural gaps and guide targeted policy interventions in community education.
