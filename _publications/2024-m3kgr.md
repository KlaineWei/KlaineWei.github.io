## Abstract

Multi-modal knowledge graphs (MMKGs) enrich recommendation by coupling entities with visual and textual signals, but prior work typically extracts the two modalities with separate pre-trained models, leading to misaligned representations and limited gains on node-similarity computation. We propose **M3KGR**, a momentum contrastive MMKG learning framework for recommendation. M3KGR leverages **CLIP** to jointly extract aligned textual and visual entity representations, and introduces a **multi-modal enhanced attention** mechanism that strengthens graph attention propagation over multi-modal neighborhoods. To mitigate data sparsity and noise that plague recommender systems, M3KGR integrates **momentum contrast** with a queue of negative samples and an instance-weighting scheme that down-weights false negatives, trained jointly with self-supervised and recommendation objectives. Experiments on three real-world benchmarks demonstrate consistent improvements over strong multi-modal and contrastive KG baselines. Code is publicly available.
